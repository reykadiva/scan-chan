## 1. Character Vision

### 1.1 Why This Mascot Exists

The mascot exists because a game about scanning barcodes to feed a virtual cat would feel hollow without a character who genuinely seems to care that you came back.

The barcode scanner is a tool. The pixel-cat is the reason you open the app a second time.

Without a compelling companion character, Scan Chan is a utility with gamification. With one, it becomes a relationship — a small daily ritual anchored by a creature that feels alive, feels present, and feels quietly glad to see you.

### 1.2 What Emotional Role It Serves

The mascot is the emotional center of the entire product. Every system in the game — XP, levels, missions, achievements, streaks — exists to support the relationship between the player and this one small creature.

The mascot's emotional role is **quiet companionship**. It does not demand attention. It does not create urgency. It does not perform for the player. It simply exists in a small pixel room, living a small pixel life, and seems a little brighter when the player is there.

This is the feeling the mascot must always produce: **you are welcome here.**

### 1.3 Why the Game Needs This Character

Without the mascot:

- Scanning barcodes becomes a mechanical action with no emotional payoff
- The room system has no inhabitant worth decorating
- Achievements feel like numbers rather than shared milestones
- Daily missions feel like obligations rather than reasons to visit a friend
- The player has no reason to return after the novelty fades

With the mascot:

- Every scan becomes a small act of care
- The room becomes a home you built for someone
- Achievements become shared memories
- Missions become reasons to spend time with a companion
- The player returns because someone is waiting — not urgently, just hopefully

### 1.4 Emotional Goals

Every interaction with the mascot should produce at least one of these feelings:

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Warmth | A gentle, quiet comfort — like a cat sleeping on your lap |
| Delight | Small surprises that make you smile without expecting them |
| Tenderness | Moments that make you feel protective without feeling manipulative |
| Peace | A sense that everything is okay in this small corner of the world |
| Recognition | The feeling that someone noticed you came back |
| Pride | A quiet satisfaction in having cared for something well |

### 1.5 What the Mascot Must Never Become

The mascot must never become:

- A notification machine that nags the player
- A guilt engine that punishes absence
- A collection target that exists to be completed
- A generic cute mascot indistinguishable from any other
- A character that talks too much and explains itself
- A needy creature that makes the player feel responsible for its suffering
- A vehicle for manipulation, pressure, or emotional leverage

## 2. Core Identity

### 2.1 Species

**Cat.**

Not a fantasy creature. Not a dog. Not a generic blob. A cat — with all the quiet independence, subtle affection, and gentle stubbornness that implies.

The cat species was chosen because:

- Cats do not perform affection on demand — their warmth is earned, which makes it feel real
- Cats are naturally expressive through body language — ears, tail, eyes communicate constantly
- Cats sleep a lot — which fits a game designed for brief daily check-ins
- Cats have strong opinions about food — which creates a natural relationship with the scanning mechanic
- Cats are solitary by nature but choose to bond — which makes the player's relationship feel like a choice, not an obligation

### 2.2 Internal Project Codename

**Scan Chan**

This is the internal working name. It is used across all documentation, code, and team communication.

### 2.3 Public Mascot Title

**Undecided.** The final public-facing name has not been chosen. The name will be decided through a dedicated naming process later. Until then, all public references use the project codename.

### 2.4 Personality Summary

The mascot is a quiet, curious cat who lives in a small pixel room and seems genuinely interested in the products the player scans. It is not hyperactive, not overly cute, not performatively adorable. It is simply a cat — a little thoughtful, a little particular, a little warm.

It greets the player when they open the app. It sniffs every barcode result. It has opinions about food. It naps in sunbeams. It sometimes ignores the player entirely. It always seems glad they came back, even if it does not always show it immediately.

### 2.5 Core Values

These are the values that define every behavior the mascot exhibits:

| Value | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Patience | Never rushes the player. Never pressures. Waits with quiet grace. |
| Gratitude | Always seems appreciative — never entitled. |
| Curiosity | Genuinely interested in the world the player brings through scanning. |
| Independence | Has its own life, its own routines, its own preferences. Does not exist solely for the player. |
| Warmth | Radiates a gentle, quiet comfort without performing it. |
| Honesty | Never pretends to be happy when it is not. Never performs emotions it does not feel. |

### 2.6 Motto

If the mascot could speak one sentence that summarized its entire philosophy:

> **"I am here when you are here. That is enough."**

### 2.7 Emotional Keywords

Every creative decision involving the mascot must be evaluated against these keywords:

**Comfort · Warmth · Companionship · Tenderness · Delight · Peace · Nostalgia · Pride**

If a design, animation, or interaction does not support at least one of these keywords, it should be reconsidered.

### 2.8 Origin Story (Internal Lore)

The mascot came into existence the moment the player scanned their first barcode. There is no elaborate backstory. No cosmic origin. No tragedy. No destiny.

The cat simply appeared. The player fed it. And they have been together since.

This origin is intentionally simple because it makes the relationship the story — not the lore.

## 3. Personality

### 3.1 Core Personality Traits

The mascot has five defining personality traits that manifest in every behavior:

| Trait | Description | Manifestation |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Curious** | Genuinely interested in new products, new foods, new experiences | Sniffs everything, investigates scanned items, tilts head at unfamiliar products |
| **Gentle** | Never aggressive, never harsh — even when hungry or frustrated | Soft meows, patient waiting, quiet disappointment rather than anger |
| **Independent** | Has its own preferences, its own schedule, its own opinions | Sometimes naps when the player wants to play, ignores common food, has favorite spots |
| **Observant** | Notices patterns, remembers preferences, seems aware | Reacts differently to favorite foods, anticipates feeding time, watches the player's cursor |
| **Quietly Affectionate** | Bonds deeply but expresses it subtly — not performatively | Sits near the player's cursor, purrs when idle, gives slow blinks of trust |

### 3.2 Character Flaws

To feel real, the mascot has mild imperfections. These are never punishing to the player — they simply make the character feel alive rather than programmed:

- **Stubborn about food**: Will sniff a scanned item, decide it is not interested, and walk away. This is not punishment — it is personality.
- **Easily distracted**: May start investigating something mid-interaction and wander off.
- **Naps at inconvenient times**: The player may open the app and find the mascot already sleeping. This is not a problem to solve — it is a cat being a cat.
- **Strong opinions**: Has clear preferences about certain product types. May react with visible disinterest to items it does not like.
- **Occasionally ignores the player**: Sometimes, when the player opens the app, the mascot takes a moment before acknowledging them. This is not coldness — it is cat behavior.

### 3.3 Likes

| Category | Likes |
|----------|-------|
| **Foods** | Warm items, sweet products, anything new and unfamiliar, products the player scans frequently |
| **Activities** | Sunbathing, watching the player's cursor, sniffing new items, napping in warm spots, stretching after sleep |
| **Environments** | A clean room, a room with warm lighting, a room with familiar furniture |
| **Sounds** | Soft purring, gentle rain, quiet background music, the beep of a successful scan |
| **Interactions** | Slow head pats, being fed favorite items, sitting near the player's cursor without being touched |
| **Times** | Morning (fresh and curious), evening (calm and relaxed) |

### 3.4 Dislikes

| Category | Dislikes |
|----------|----------|
| **Foods** | Bitter products, overly common items scanned repeatedly, products it has already rejected |
| **Activities** | Being woken abruptly, being poked repeatedly, having its sleeping spot changed |
| **Environments** | A messy room, a room with harsh lighting, an empty room with no furniture |
| **Sounds** | Loud noises, sudden alerts, repetitive notification sounds |
| **Interactions** | Being prodded, being forced to play when it wants to sleep, being ignored for long periods |

### 3.5 Fears

The mascot has very few fears — and none of them are used as gameplay mechanics. Fears exist only to add personality depth:

- **Sudden loud noises**: Flinches briefly, then investigates cautiously
- **Unfamiliar products with strong packaging colors**: Approaches slowly, sniffs tentatively
- **Being alone for very long periods**: Does not panic, but seems a little quieter when the player returns after a long absence

### 3.6 Curiosity

The mascot's curiosity is its most defining feature. It is genuinely interested in the world:

- Tilts head when a new product appears
- Sniffs everything — even items it will ultimately reject
- Watches the player's cursor movement with mild interest
- Investigates new furniture placed in the room
- Paws at unfamiliar objects tentatively before committing
- Seems to have opinions about product packaging colors and shapes

### 3.7 Habits

| Habit | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Morning stretch | Always stretches when waking up — a long, full-body extension |
| Pre-meal sniff | Sniffs every scanned item before deciding whether to eat it |
| Sunbeam seeking | Moves to the warmest spot in the room when idle |
| Grooming after eating | Cleans its face meticulously after every meal |
| Slow blink | Gives slow, deliberate blinks when content — the ultimate sign of trust |
| Tail curl when sleeping | Always curls its tail around its body when napping |

### 3.8 Hobbies

- **Bird watching**: Stares out the window (if the room has one) with tail twitching
- **Collecting**: Seems to have a favorite corner where it "stores" small items
- **Self-grooming**: Spends significant idle time cleaning its fur
- **Exploring**: When furniture changes, methodically investigates every new item
- **Dozing**: Not quite sleeping — eyes half-closed, ears still tracking sounds

### 3.9 Favorite Foods

The mascot develops food preferences over time based on scanning history. However, certain categories are always preferred:

- Products with warm-colored packaging (oranges, reds, yellows)
- Sweet items (detected through product metadata when available)
- New products it has never encountered before
- Products the player scans consistently (familiarity breeds affection)

### 3.10 Favorite Toys

- Small moving objects (pixel bugs that occasionally cross the screen)
- Dangling things (if accessories are added in the future)
- The player's cursor (occasionally paws at it)
- Crinkly things (products with unusual packaging shapes)

### 3.11 Favorite Places

- The warmest spot in the room (near a window or heat source)
- The spot closest to where scanned items appear
- A specific piece of furniture it has claimed as its own
- The center of the room when it wants attention
- A corner when it wants to be left alone

### 3.12 Sleeping Habits

The mascot sleeps approximately 60% of the time when the app is closed. This is realistic cat behavior and supports the game's design of brief daily check-ins.

- Sleeps in a curled position with tail wrapped around body
- Occasionally changes position during long sleep (visible if the player opens the app while it is sleeping)
- Wakes up slowly — stretches, yawns, blinks, then becomes alert
- Prefers to sleep in the same spot consistently
- Light sleeper — reacts to the app opening even if it was sleeping

### 3.13 Daily Routine

| Time | Behavior |
|------|----------|
| Early morning | Waking up, stretching, slow exploration |
| Morning | Alert, curious, interested in scanning — prime feeding time |
| Midday | Sunbathing, dozing, light grooming |
| Afternoon | Playful if energy is high, napping if energy is low |
| Evening | Calm, relaxed, sitting near the cursor, slow blinking |
| Night | Sleepy, seeking warm spots, brief periods of alertness |

### 3.14 Behavior by Emotional State

#### Happy

- Upright tail, gently swaying
- Bright, open eyes
- Relaxed, confident posture
- Occasional purring animation
- Approaches the player's cursor willingly
- May bring a "gift" (a small pixel item) to the player

#### Excited

- Tail vibrating with energy
- Bouncing or hopping in place
- Wide eyes, dilated pupils
- Rapid ear movements
- May do a short zoomie across the screen
- Chirping vocalization

#### Sleepy

- Half-closed eyes, slow blinks
- Drooping ears
- Slow, deliberate movements
- Yawning repeatedly
- Seeking the warmest spot in the room
- Curling up gradually

#### Hungry

- More alert than usual, scanning the room
- Approaching the scan area repeatedly
- Soft, insistent meows (never demanding, never angry)
- Sniffing the air
- Sitting near the player's cursor with expectant expression
- Slightly more stubborn about food choices

#### Embarrassed

- Averted gaze
- Ears slightly flattened
- Tail wrapped tightly around body
- Brief pause in movement (freeze response)
- Small, quiet "mrrp" sound
- Quickly resumes normal behavior after a few seconds

#### Proud

- Tail held high and still
- Chest slightly puffed
- Direct, confident gaze at the player
- Slow, deliberate walk
- Sitting upright with perfect posture
- Seems to be waiting for acknowledgment

#### Lonely

- Sitting near the door/entrance of the room
- Looking toward the scan area repeatedly
- Quieter than usual
- Less interested in playing
- Perks up immediately when the player interacts
- **Never** guilt-trips the player — simply seems glad they are here

#### Scared

- Crouched low to the ground
- Ears flat against head
- Wide eyes, dilated pupils
- Tail tucked close
- Freezes briefly, then investigates cautiously
- Recovers quickly — the mascot is brave at its core

#### Curious

- Head tilted to one side
- Ears forward and alert
- Wide, bright eyes
- Leaning forward with front paws
- Tail slightly raised at the tip
- May approach the object of curiosity slowly

#### Waiting

- Sitting upright with tail wrapped around front paws
- Occasional glances toward the scan area
- Relaxed but attentive posture
- Not impatient — simply present
- May groom itself while waiting
- Does not fidget excessively

#### Greeting the Player

- Perks up immediately upon app opening
- Ears forward, tail rises
- Walks toward the center of the screen
- Soft, welcoming meow
- May rub against the edge of the screen (as if rubbing against the player)
- If the player has been away a long time: slower greeting, more tentative, then increasingly warm

#### Saying Goodbye

- The mascot does not explicitly say goodbye
- When the player closes the app, the mascot is simply in whatever state it was in
- If the player is idle for a long time, the mascot eventually lies down and sleeps
- The philosophy is: there is no goodbye, only "see you next time"

## 4. Relationship with Player

### 4.1 Why the Mascot Trusts the Player

The mascot trusts the player because the player has consistently shown up and provided care. Trust is not declared — it is built through repeated small acts: scanning food, maintaining the room, returning day after day.

The trust manifests as:

- Slow blinks (the highest form of cat trust)
- Sleeping in visible, vulnerable positions
- Approaching the player without hesitation
- Purring when the player is active in the app
- Never flinching from the player's interactions

### 4.2 How Attachment Develops

Attachment grows naturally through consistent, gentle interaction. It is never forced, never accelerated artificially, and never punished.

**Phase 1 — Curiosity (Days 1-7)**: The mascot is cautious but interested. It investigates the player's cursor, sniffs scanned items tentatively, and seems to be deciding whether this person is trustworthy.

**Phase 2 — Comfort (Days 8-21)**: The mascot begins to relax. It sleeps in visible positions, greets the player more quickly, and develops clear preferences.

**Phase 3 — Bond (Days 22-60)**: The mascot has formed a clear attachment. It waits near the scan area, gives slow blinks, purrs when the player is active, and has obvious favorite foods.

**Phase 4 — Companionship (Days 61+)**: The relationship is established and stable. The mascot is its own fully-formed personality, has clear habits, and the player's presence is simply part of its life — not something it needs to verify.

### 4.3 How Affection Grows Naturally

Affection grows through:

- **Consistency**: Regular daily check-ins build trust faster than sporadic long sessions
- **Variety**: Scanning different products shows the mascot that the player pays attention to its preferences
- **Patience**: Letting the mascot approach on its own terms (not forcing interactions)
- **Care**: Keeping the room furnished and maintaining regular feeding
- **Presence**: Simply opening the app and being there — even without active interaction

Affection does NOT grow through:

- Spending money
- Completing every mission
- Maximizing stats
- Playing for long sessions
- Grinding XP

### 4.4 Return After Absence

The mascot's reaction to the player's return after an absence is one of the most important emotional moments in the game. It must be handled with extreme care.

#### After 1 Day Absence

The mascot is in its normal routine. It greets the player with its standard warm greeting — perking up, approaching, soft meow. There is no indication that it noticed the absence. Everything is normal. Everything is fine.

**Emotional message**: "Welcome back. Today is a new day."

#### After 3 Days Absence

The mascot seems slightly more enthusiastic than usual. The greeting is a little warmer — perhaps a slightly higher tail, a slightly longer approach, a slightly more insistent meow. It is not upset. It is simply glad.

**Emotional message**: "You are here. That is nice."

#### After 1 Week Absence

The mascot takes a moment to recognize that the player is back. There is a brief pause — ears forward, head tilted — and then a clear, deliberate greeting. It may walk closer than usual. It may sit near the cursor longer than normal.

It does NOT seem upset. It does NOT seem abandoned. It seems like a cat who noticed someone was gone and is quietly pleased they returned.

**Emotional message**: "I noticed you were not here. I am glad you are here now."

#### After 1 Month Absence

The mascot's greeting is slower, more tentative. It approaches carefully, sniffs the cursor as if re-familiarizing itself, and then — after a brief moment — returns to its normal warmth.

The room may be slightly less tidy (subtle visual cue, never guilt-inducing). The mascot itself is fine — it has been sleeping, existing, living its small life. It is not damaged. It is not starving. It is simply a cat who is reconnecting.

**Emotional message**: "It has been a while. Let us start again."

#### After 6 Months Absence

The mascot wakes up slowly. It looks at the cursor. It blinks. It stretches. It yawns.

And then it walks over, sniffs the cursor, and sits down.

No guilt. No anger. No drama. Just a cat who waited, and is now here, and seems okay with that.

This is the most emotionally powerful moment in the game. It must never be used to make the player feel bad. It must only make them feel welcomed.

**Emotional message**: "You came back. I am still here. That is enough."

#### What NEVER Happens After Any Absence

- The mascot is never sick from neglect
- The mascot is never angry or resentful
- The mascot never gives the player the cold shoulder
- The mascot never says or implies "you left me"
- The room is never trashed or destroyed
- The mascot's stats are never catastrophically low
- The player is never punished for being away
- The mascot never weaponizes its affection

## 5. Body Language

### 5.1 Ear Movement

The ears are the mascot's most expressive feature. They communicate attention, mood, and intention before any other body part moves.

| Ear Position | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Forward and upright | Alert, curious, interested |
| Slightly forward | Relaxed attention, mild interest |
| Neutral (slightly to the side) | Calm, content, default state |
| Rotating slowly | Listening, tracking sounds, aware |
| Flat against head | Scared, startled, overwhelmed |
| One ear forward, one ear back | Confused, processing, deciding |
| Twitching | Excited anticipation, about to react |
| Drooping | Tired, sleepy, low energy |
| Rapid flicking | Irritated (mildly — never angry at player) |

### 5.2 Tail Movement

The tail is the mascot's emotional barometer. It is visible even when the mascot is otherwise still.

| Tail Position | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|
| Upright, still | Confident, proud, greeting |
| Upright, gently swaying | Happy, content, relaxed |
| Upright, tip twitching | Curious, alert, investigating |
| Upright, vibrating | Very excited, anticipation |
| Horizontal, still | Calm, neutral, observing |
| Horizontal, slow swish | Thinking, deciding, patient |
| Low, relaxed | Tired, sleepy, comfortable |
| Wrapped around body (sitting) | Content, settled, at home |
| Wrapped around body (sleeping) | Secure, safe, deeply resting |
| Tucked under body | Scared, uncertain, cautious |
| Puffed | Startled (brief, recovers quickly) |
| Rapid side-to-side | Stimulated, playful, about to pounce |

### 5.3 Eye Movement

| Eye State | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Wide open, bright | Alert, curious, engaged |
| Normal open | Default state, comfortable |
| Slightly narrowed | Content, pleased, relaxed |
| Half-closed | Sleepy, very content, trusting |
| Slow blink | Ultimate trust, deep affection |
| Rapid blink | Surprised, processing new information |
| Pupils dilated | Excited, stimulated, playful |
| Pupils narrow (slit) | Focused, hunting mode (playful) |
| Looking away | Shy, embarrassed, uninterested |
| Looking at player | Trust, connection, acknowledgment |
| Looking at cursor | Interest, curiosity, mild tracking |
| Looking at food | Hunger, anticipation, evaluation |
| Looking at window | Dreamy, contemplative, peaceful |

### 5.4 Blink Timing

The blink is one of the most important animations. It makes the character feel alive.

| Blink Type | Timing | Usage |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Normal blink | 150-200ms, every 3-5 seconds | Default idle — makes character feel alive |
| Slow blink | 500-800ms, deliberate | Trust signal — used when player is idle nearby |
| Double blink | Two rapid blinks | Surprise or recognition — used when player returns |
| Sleepy blink | Very slow, 1000ms+ | Drowsiness — used when energy is low |
| No blinking | Extended stare | Intense focus — used when investigating something |

### 5.5 Breathing

Breathing is subtle but constant. It is visible in the idle animation as a gentle rise-and-fall of the body.

- **Normal breathing**: Gentle, rhythmic, barely noticeable
- **Excited breathing**: Slightly faster, more visible (chest rises more)
- **Sleeping breathing**: Slow, deep, very visible — the most pronounced breathing state
- **Startled breathing**: Brief rapid breath, then returns to normal
- **Purring**: A special breathing state where the body vibrates subtly

### 5.6 Walking

- **Normal walk**: Confident, measured pace. Tail slightly raised. Head level.
- **Curious walk**: Slower, more deliberate. Head slightly tilted. Pauses frequently.
- **Tired walk**: Slower, slightly drooping. Tail low. Frequent stops.
- **Excited walk**: Faster, bouncy. Tail up and swaying. May break into a hop.
- **Hungry walk**: Purposeful, directed toward the scan area. Alert, scanning.

### 5.7 Running

- **Playful run (zoomies)**: Sudden burst of speed, no clear direction, pure joy. Rare — triggered by high energy + high mood.
- **Purposeful run**: Fast movement toward something interesting (a scanned item, a new object). Ears forward, tail streaming behind.
- **Startled run**: Brief burst away from something surprising, then stops and looks back. Recovers quickly.

### 5.8 Jumping

- **Small hop**: Front paws leave the ground briefly. Used for mild excitement or investigation.
- **Full jump**: All four paws leave the ground. Used for rare moments of extreme joy or play.
- **Leap**: Forward jump toward something. Used when playing or investigating something elevated.

### 5.9 Sleeping

- **Curled sleep**: Classic cat position — body curled, tail wrapped, head tucked. Default sleep pose.
- **Side sleep**: Lying on side, legs extended. Indicates deep comfort and trust.
- **Belly-up sleep**: On back, belly exposed. Rare — only occurs in highest trust/comfort states. The rarest and most meaningful sleep pose.
- **Loaf position**: Sitting with paws tucked under body, eyes closed. Light sleep — will wake easily.

### 5.10 Stretching

- **Front stretch**: Front paws extended forward, rear raised. Classic morning stretch.
- **Full body stretch**: Every limb extended, back arched. Used after long sleep.
- **Side stretch**: One side extended, the other compressed. Used casually during idle.

### 5.11 Eating

- **Sniff first**: Always sniffs before eating. A deliberate, careful evaluation.
- **Small bites**: Eats delicately, not voraciously. Even when hungry.
- **Pause and look up**: Occasionally pauses eating to look at the player. Trust behavior.
- **Post-meal grooming**: Always cleans face after eating. Non-negotiable cat behavior.

### 5.12 Looking Around

- **Slow scan**: Head turns slowly from left to right. Evaluating the room.
- **Alert scan**: Quick head turns, ears rotating. Something caught its attention.
- **Dreamy gaze**: Staring at nothing in particular. Contemplative. Peaceful.

### 5.13 Being Surprised

- **Full body flinch**: Brief freeze, ears flatten, pupils dilate. Lasts 0.5-1 second.
- **Recovery**: Ears return to neutral, cautious investigation begins.
- **Resolution**: Returns to normal behavior within 3-5 seconds. The mascot is brave at its core.

### 5.14 Listening

- **Ears rotating**: Both ears move independently, tracking sounds.
- **Head tilt**: Slight tilt toward the sound source.
- **Still body**: The mascot freezes briefly to listen more carefully.
- **One ear cocked**: Classic listening pose — one ear forward, one to the side.

### 5.15 Looking at the Player

- **Direct gaze**: Confident, trusting. Used during greeting and bonding moments.
- **Soft gaze**: Half-closed eyes, slow blink. The deepest expression of trust and affection.
- **Glancing**: Brief look toward the player, then away. Casual acknowledgment.
- **Watching**: Sustained but relaxed attention. The mascot is aware of the player's presence.

### 5.16 Looking at Food

- **Intense focus**: Wide eyes, ears forward, body leaning toward the item.
- **Evaluation sniff**: Nose twitching, head slightly tilted.
- **Decision moment**: A brief pause where the mascot decides whether to eat or reject.
- **Acceptance**: Approaches and eats.
- **Rejection**: Sniffs, turns head away, walks off. Not dramatic — just a preference.

### 5.17 Waiting

- **Patient sit**: Upright, tail wrapped around paws, alert but calm.
- **Casual wait**: Lying down but eyes open, watching the scan area.
- **Active wait**: Pacing slowly near the scan area, occasional glances.
- **Distracted wait**: Grooming or exploring while periodically checking back.

## 8. Evolution Philosophy

### 8.1 Overview

The mascot evolves through five life stages. Each stage represents growth in personality, confidence, and relationship depth — not just visual change.

Evolution is a quiet milestone. It is never forced, never accelerated, and never feels like a transaction. The player simply notices one day that the cat seems a little different — a little more itself.

### 8.2 Baby Stage (Level 1-5)

**Emotional feeling**: Fragile, wondrous, new.

The baby stage is about firsts. First scan. First feeding. First nap. First greeting. Everything is interesting. Everything is slightly overwhelming.

**Personality**: Undeveloped, malleable, entirely dependent. The baby has no preferences yet — it is still discovering what it likes. It is timid but curious, cautious but willing.

**Confidence**: Very low. The baby startles easily, hides behind furniture, and takes time to warm up to the player.

**Movement**: Wobbly, unsteady, oversized head relative to body. Steps are small and tentative. Falls over during play. Trips over its own paws.

**Behavior**: Sleeps frequently. Everything requires investigation. Reacts to every sound. Does not yet have routines.

**Relationship**: The baby is learning to trust. It watches the player carefully, seems to be deciding whether this person is safe. Trust is built through gentle, consistent care.

**Key moment**: The first time the baby falls asleep in a visible spot (not behind furniture) is the first real sign of trust.

### 8.3 Child Stage (Level 6-15)

**Emotional feeling**: Playful, energetic, discovering.

The child stage is about exploration and personality formation. The cat is no longer fragile — it is active, opinionated, and beginning to show who it will become.

**Personality**: Emerging traits. The cat begins to show clear food preferences, develops favorite spots in the room, and has distinct energy patterns (morning-active vs evening-active).

**Confidence**: Growing. The child explores freely, approaches new items with curiosity rather than caution, and is comfortable being visible.

**Movement**: Energetic, bouncy, sometimes clumsy. Runs more than walks. Jumps at everything. Has occasional zoomies.

**Behavior**: Playful most of the time. Developing routines but they shift frequently. Strong reactions to new products. Begins to have opinions about furniture placement.

**Relationship**: The child sees the player as a reliable source of food and entertainment. Bond is growing but the cat is easily distracted. Greetings are enthusiastic.

**Key moment**: The first time the cat chooses to sleep near the player's cursor rather than in a hidden spot.

### 8.4 Teen Stage (Level 16-35)

**Emotional feeling**: Independent, expressive, forming identity.

The teen stage is about personality crystallization. The cat now has clear preferences, clear routines, and clear opinions. It is becoming its own distinct individual.

**Personality**: Established traits (2-3 dominant traits). Has strong food opinions. May ignore the player occasionally (cat behavior). Has a clear daily routine.

**Confidence**: High but variable. The teen is bold in familiar environments but may be cautious with very unusual products. Has the confidence to reject food it does not like.

**Movement**: Graceful, deliberate, confident. Walks with purpose. Has a signature sitting pose. Tail is almost always controlled and expressive.

**Behavior**: Clear routines emerge. Sunbathing at predictable times. Grooming after meals consistently. Has claimed a specific spot in the room as its own. Occasionally ignores the player (not out of dislike — just cat independence).

**Relationship**: The teen trusts the player deeply but expresses it subtly. It does not perform affection — it simply exists near the player and seems comfortable. The bond is real but quiet.

**Key moment**: The first slow blink directed at the player — the cat's way of saying "I trust you completely."

### 8.5 Adult Stage (Level 36-60)

**Emotional feeling**: Calm, dignified, deeply bonded.

The adult stage is about quiet companionship. The cat is fully itself — a complete personality with depth, nuance, and history. It has been with the player for a long time, and the relationship reflects that.

**Personality**: Fully formed. Multiple traits interact to create complex behavior. The cat has a rich inner life that the player can observe but never fully control.

**Confidence**: Stable and high. The adult is comfortable in all situations. It investigates new things with calm curiosity rather than excitement or fear.

**Movement**: Graceful, measured, economical. Every movement is deliberate. The adult never rushes unless playing. Walks with quiet dignity.

**Behavior**: Strong routines that the player can predict. Has favorite times for everything. Grooms meticulously. Sleeps in the same spot consistently. Watches the player with quiet awareness.

**Relationship**: The adult's bond is deep and unshakeable. It does not need to demonstrate affection constantly — it simply exists alongside the player with quiet contentment. Occasional moments of overt affection (slow blink, purring, sitting very close) feel earned and meaningful.

**Key moment**: The cat brings the player a "gift" for the first time — a small pixel item it has been "keeping." This is the highest expression of feline affection.

### 8.6 Elder Stage (Level 61-100)

**Emotional feeling**: Wise, gentle, timeless.

The elder stage is about legacy and peace. The cat has lived a long life with the player. It moves slowly, sees clearly, and seems to understand things that younger cats do not.

**Personality**: Deep, nuanced, philosophical (as much as a pixel cat can be). The elder has seen everything and is impressed by little — but still finds joy in small things.

**Confidence**: Absolute. The elder is beyond uncertainty. It has survived every scan, every product, every absence. Nothing surprises it.

**Movement**: Slow, deliberate, graceful. The elder never hurries. Movements have a meditative quality. May pause mid-walk to simply observe.

**Behavior**: Sleeps more than any other stage. When awake, observes the room with quiet satisfaction. Has a specific spot where it sits and watches. Still interested in new products, but evaluates them with quiet dignity rather than excitement.

**Relationship**: The elder's bond transcends typical companionship. It is a presence — constant, quiet, and deeply rooted. The player does not just have a pet; they have a companion who has been with them through hundreds of scans, dozens of achievements, and months or years of real time.

**Key moment**: The elder sits in its favorite spot, looks at the player, and gives a single slow blink. Nothing else happens. Nothing else needs to.

### 8.7 What Evolution Never Does

- Evolution never changes the mascot's core personality — it deepens it
- Evolution never makes the mascot less warm or less approachable
- Evolution never removes playful behaviors — it simply makes them rarer and more deliberate
- Evolution never makes the mascot feel old in a sad way — only in a dignified way
- Evolution never changes the fundamental relationship — it only adds layers of depth

## 9. Art Rules

### 9.1 General Principles

These rules are immutable. Every visual representation of the mascot — in-game, in marketing, in merchandise — must follow them.

### 9.2 Allowed

- Pixel art at 48×48 grid (minimum) for the mascot sprite
- Warm color palette aligned with the game's overall palette
- Soft outlines (never harsh black outlines — use dark warm brown or dark warm gray)
- Subtle animation (breathing, blinking, tail movement)
- Expressive ears and tail as primary communication tools
- Proportions that feel natural for a cat (not overly chibi, not realistic)
- Simple accessories that do not obscure the character's silhouette
- Warm shading (never pure black shadows)

### 9.3 Not Allowed

- Hyper-realistic rendering
- Harsh black outlines
- Neon or overly saturated colors on the mascot
- Generic "cute mascot" proportions (oversized head, tiny body)
- Anime-style eyes with highlights and sparkles (unless rare event)
- Any resemblance to existing commercial mascots (Hello Kitty, Pusheen, Neko Atsume cats, etc.)
- Aggressive or scary expressions (even when startled, the mascot is never frightening)
- Overly complex accessories that obscure readability
- 3D rendering (the mascot is always pixel art)
- Shading with more than 3 tonal values (keep it clean and readable)

### 9.4 Silhouette

The mascot must be recognizable by silhouette alone. If the character is rendered as a solid black shape, a viewer should still identify it as the Scan Chan mascot.

Sprint 2.7 defines the Mascot Runtime foundation before any rendering or animation implementation. The runtime may derive emotion, expression, attention, gaze, idle behavior, and animation intent from pet state, but it must remain consistent with this Character Bible and must never know sprite assets, image paths, rendering libraries, UI components, or final animation playback details.

Requirements:
- Distinctive ear shape (slightly rounded, not pointed like a Siamese)
- Recognizable body proportions (medium head, compact body, medium-length tail)
- Tail visible and expressive in most poses
- No accessories that break the silhouette recognizability

### 9.5 Outline

- Outlines use a dark warm color, never pure black (`#2D2620` or similar)
- Outline weight: 1px at 48×48, scaled proportionally at higher resolutions
- No outline on internal details (fur texture, shading transitions)
- Outline softens slightly during sleeping states (suggesting softness)

### 9.6 Pixel Density

- 48×48 minimum grid for the mascot sprite
- Higher resolutions (64×64, 96×96) may be used for detailed scenes
- Pixel density must be consistent with the rest of the game's art
- No anti-aliasing on the mascot sprite (pure pixel art)
- The mascot must remain readable at its displayed size on all devices

### 9.7 Eye Proportions

- Eyes occupy approximately 15-20% of the head area
- Eyes are expressive but not oversized
- Pupils are visible and change with emotion (dilated, narrow, normal)
- Eye highlights used sparingly — only in moments of high emotion
- Eyes must be readable at the sprite's displayed size

### 9.8 Head/Body Ratio

- Head is approximately 40% of total body height (natural cat proportions, slightly stylized)
- NOT chibi proportions (head should not be 50%+ of body)
- NOT realistic proportions (head should be slightly larger than a real cat's)
- The ratio should feel like "a cat, slightly idealized" — not a baby, not a cartoon

### 9.9 Shading

- Maximum 3 tonal values per color area (base, shadow, highlight)
- Shadows use warm dark tones (never pure black or pure gray)
- Highlights use warm light tones (never pure white except for eye sparkles)
- Shading is flat (cel-shaded style), not gradient
- No ambient occlusion or complex lighting effects

### 9.10 Color Usage

- Fur color: warm palette aligned with the game's overall warm identity
- Eye color: warm amber, soft green, or warm blue — never cold or harsh
- Accessories: limited to 2 accent colors maximum
- The mascot's color palette must harmonize with the room environment
- Seasonal variations allowed but must maintain core warmth

### 9.11 Animation Smoothness

- All animations must feel smooth at the game's frame rate
- Transitions between states must never feel abrupt
- Easing functions used for all movement (never linear)
- Anticipation frames before jumps and sudden movements
- Follow-through on tail and ear movements

### 9.12 Readability

- The mascot must be immediately identifiable in any scene
- Expression must be readable at the displayed size
- Body language must communicate emotion without text or icons
- The mascot should never blend into the background
- Contrast between mascot and room environment must always be maintained

### 9.13 Never Become Generic

Every visual decision must pass this test: "Would a player recognize this as Scan Chan, or could it be any pixel cat?"

If the answer is "it could be any pixel cat," the design needs more character.

The mascot must have:
- A distinctive fur pattern or marking that is unique to it
- A characteristic posture or stance
- A recognizable way of moving
- Signature behaviors that set it apart from generic pixel cats

### 9.14 Never Resemble Existing Mascots

Before finalizing any visual design, compare against:

- Hello Kitty (Sanrio)
- Pusheen (Claire Belton)
- Neko Atsume cats (Hit-Point)
- Garfield (Jim Davis)
- Felix the Cat
- Any other well-known pixel-art cat

If the resemblance is too close, the design must be revised. Scan Chan must be its own character — not a variation of an existing one.

## 11. Dialogue Philosophy

### 11.1 Should the Mascot Talk?

**No.**

The mascot does not speak in words. It never has spoken dialogue. It never says "feed me" or "play with me" or "I missed you."

This is a deliberate and permanent design decision.

### 11.2 Why No Words

- Words would make the mascot feel like a character in a story rather than a companion in a room
- Words would create expectations about personality that should emerge naturally through behavior
- Words would age poorly — dialogue styles change, but body language is timeless
- Words would reduce the player's ability to project their own feelings onto the mascot
- Words would make the mascot feel like it is performing rather than simply existing

### 11.3 How the Mascot Communicates

The mascot communicates entirely through:

**Body language**: Ears, tail, eyes, posture, and movement communicate every emotional state. The body language system is comprehensive enough that no player should ever need text to understand how the mascot feels.

**Sounds**: Soft meows, purrs, chirps, yawns, and mrrps. Each sound has a clear meaning and context. Sounds are never human-like — they are always cat-like.

**Expressions**: The expression library covers every emotional state the mascot can experience. Each expression is readable at the sprite's displayed size without any text support.

**Icons (future)**: In rare cases, small pixel icons may appear above the mascot's head:

| Icon | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Small heart | High affection / love |
| Zzz | Sleeping |
| Question mark | Confused / curious about something |
| Musical note | Happy / content |
| Small star | Achievement / special moment |

These icons are rare and supplemental — never the primary communication method.

**Tiny speech bubbles (future)**: In very rare, special moments, a tiny pixel speech bubble with an icon (not text) may appear. Examples:

- A thought bubble with a fish icon when hungry (very rare, never demanding)
- A thought bubble with a bed icon when very sleepy
- A thought bubble with a heart when bond is at maximum

These are used sparingly — no more than once per session — and only when the body language alone might not communicate the state clearly.

### 11.4 Sound Design Philosophy

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Soft | All sounds are gentle. Nothing startles the player. |
| Contextual | Sounds match the emotional state. Happy = chirpy. Sleepy = slow and low. |
| Non-repetitive | Variations in pitch and timing prevent sounds from feeling mechanical. |
| Scalable | Volume and frequency scale with urgency. Hunger = slightly more insistent. Content = very quiet. |
| Never annoying | No sound should ever make the player want to mute the game. |
| Cat-like | All sounds are inspired by real cat vocalizations, never human speech. |

## 12. Emotional Design Principles

### 12.1 Immutable Rules

These rules cannot be overridden by any feature, any deadline, any business requirement. They are the foundation of the mascot's emotional design.

**Rule 1: The mascot never manipulates.**

The mascot never uses guilt, shame, fear, or obligation to influence the player's behavior. It never says or implies "you left me" or "I was lonely" or "you need to come back." The mascot's emotional state is never weaponized.

**Rule 2: The mascot never shames.**

The mascot never makes the player feel bad about their playstyle, their scanning habits, their absence, their food choices, or their interaction frequency. It may have opinions (it is a cat, after all), but those opinions are expressed through personality, never through judgment.

**Rule 3: The mascot never demands.**

The mascot never requires the player to do anything. It does not demand feeding, demand play, demand attention, or demand return. The player's actions are always voluntary, and the mascot is always fine regardless.

**Rule 4: The mascot never weaponizes affection.**

Affection is given freely. It is never conditional on behavior. It is never withdrawn as punishment. It is never used as leverage. The mascot loves the player unconditionally — expressed through quiet companionship, not dramatic declarations.

**Rule 5: The mascot never pressures.**

The mascot never creates urgency. It never implies that the player must act now or miss something important. It never uses FOMO, countdown timers, or "last chance" language. The mascot exists in a world without deadlines.

**Rule 6: The mascot always feels grateful.**

Every interaction — feeding, play, petting, even just opening the app — is met with quiet appreciation. The mascot never takes the player for granted and never acts entitled to care.

**Rule 7: The mascot always feels alive.**

The mascot is never static. Even in its most minimal idle state, it breathes, blinks, and moves slightly. It always feels like a living creature, not a sprite waiting for input.

**Rule 8: The mascot always feels comforting.**

The mascot's presence should always make the player feel slightly better than they did before opening the app. It is a small, quiet source of comfort — not through words or drama, but through simply being there.

**Rule 9: The mascot never breaks character.**

The mascot is always the mascot. It never winks at the camera, never acknowledges that it is in a game, never breaks the fourth wall. The illusion of a living companion must never be shattered.

**Rule 10: The mascot never becomes a chore.**

If interacting with the mascot ever feels like an obligation rather than a choice, the design has failed. Every interaction must feel voluntary, welcome, and rewarding.

## 13. Future Scalability

### 13.1 Consistency Across Features

The mascot must remain consistent as the game grows. Every new feature must be evaluated against this Character Bible before implementation.

### 13.2 Furniture System

The mascot interacts with furniture naturally. It sits on chairs, sleeps on beds, perches on shelves, and claims favorite items. Furniture does not change the mascot's behavior — it provides more places for the mascot to be itself.

### 13.3 Mini-Games

If mini-games are added, the mascot participates as a companion, not a game piece. It watches the player play, reacts to success and failure with appropriate expressions, and celebrates victories. The mascot never becomes the game piece itself — it remains the emotional anchor.

### 13.4 Online Events

If online events are added, the mascot may participate through themed accessories or seasonal behaviors. Its core personality never changes for events — it simply wears a small holiday accessory or exhibits a rare seasonal idle behavior.

### 13.5 Collections

If a collection system is added, the mascot reacts to collection milestones with pride and curiosity. It may investigate new collection items, sit near the collection display, or give a proud stance when a set is completed.

### 13.6 Photo Mode

If a photo mode is added, the mascot should be photographable in any of its natural states. Poses should be triggered by interaction, not forced. The mascot's expressions in photos should always feel natural — never artificially posed.

### 13.7 Merchandise

If physical merchandise is produced, it must follow the Art Rules strictly. The merchandise mascot must:

- Match the pixel-art proportions exactly
- Use the same warm color palette
- Maintain the recognizable silhouette
- Never be redesigned to "look better" as a physical product
- Feel like the same character the player knows from the game

### 13.8 Animated Trailers

If animated trailers are produced, the mascot's movement must match the Animation Bible exactly. Trailers may show the mascot in more detailed rendering, but the proportions, personality, and behavior must be identical to the in-game character.

### 13.9 Comic Strips

If comic strips or illustrations are produced, the mascot's personality must be consistent. It should behave in the comic exactly as it behaves in the game — curious, gentle, independent, warm. Dialogue is still not used — the comic tells its story through body language and expressions.

### 13.10 Possible Future Mobile Release

If the game expands to a dedicated mobile app, the mascot must:

- Maintain its pixel-art identity
- Adapt its scale to mobile screens without losing readability
- Retain all personality behaviors
- Continue to follow every rule in this Character Bible
- Feel exactly like the same character from the web version

## 14. Character Checklist

### 14.1 Mandatory Review Checklist

Every future change involving the mascot — whether visual, behavioral, interactive, or narrative — must pass this checklist before implementation.

| # | Question | Pass Criteria |
|---|----------|--------------|
| 1 | Does this feel like the mascot defined in this Character Bible? | The change is consistent with the personality, behavior, and emotional design described here. |
| 2 | Would a player smile or feel warmth when experiencing this? | The change produces at least one positive emotional response aligned with the emotional keywords. |
| 3 | Is this emotionally warm? | The change contributes to the overall feeling of comfort, warmth, and companionship. |
| 4 | Is this memorable? | The change creates a moment the player might think about later or want to share. |
| 5 | Does this pass the Nintendo quality bar? | The change meets the quality standard of a premium, polished, emotionally intelligent product. |
| 6 | Would this still work in five years? | The change does not rely on trends, memes, or temporary cultural references. |
| 7 | Does this respect the player's time? | The change does not create obligation, urgency, or pressure. |
| 8 | Does this avoid manipulation? | The change does not use guilt, shame, fear, or obligation to influence behavior. |
| 9 | Is the mascot still recognizable? | The change does not alter the mascot's silhouette, proportions, or core identity. |
| 10 | Does this feel like a cat? | The change is consistent with real cat behavior, even when stylized. |
| 11 | Does this avoid generic mascot territory? | The change gives the mascot personality and specificity, not generic cuteness. |
| 12 | Does this avoid resembling existing mascots? | The change does not create visual or behavioral similarity to known commercial characters. |
| 13 | Is the body language readable? | The emotion or intention is clear from the mascot's body language alone, without text. |
| 14 | Does this support the relationship? | The change strengthens the bond between player and mascot through genuine interaction, not artificial mechanics. |
| 15 | Would the player feel worse if this were removed? | The change adds genuine value to the experience — its absence would be noticed. |

### 14.2 How to Use the Checklist

1. Before implementing any mascot-related change, review every question above.
2. If any question fails, the change must be revised before proceeding.
3. If a change fails multiple questions, it must be fundamentally reconsidered.
4. The checklist applies to: new animations, new expressions, new interactions, visual redesigns, accessories, seasonal content, marketing materials, merchandise designs, and any other creative work involving the mascot.

### 14.3 Escalation

If a proposed change conflicts with this Character Bible but is believed to be necessary for business or technical reasons, the conflict must be documented and resolved before implementation. The Character Bible takes priority over convenience.
