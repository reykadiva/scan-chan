# Creative Review Checklists

Use these checklists after loading the governing documents from `design-document-router.md`. These are review gates, not replacements for the source documents.

## UI Layout

- Pet-first hierarchy is preserved where the pet is relevant.
- Layout follows UI Production Guide composition, spacing, radius, elevation, responsive, and component rules.
- Surface avoids dashboard-first, admin, CRUD, or generic SaaS patterns.
- Design tokens are used for color, spacing, type, radius, shadow, and motion.
- Visual hierarchy guides attention without clutter or feature density.

## UX Flow

- Flow supports the emotional goal defined in the Player Experience Document.
- Primary action is clear without pressure, urgency, or manipulation.
- Player feedback is immediate, warm, and recoverable.
- Flow does not turn care into a chore or checklist.
- Copy follows Brand Book voice.

## Animation And Motion

- Motion has a purpose: feedback, continuity, emotional expression, state change, or attention.
- Timing and easing follow Visual Design and UI Production motion guidance.
- Motion is gentle; nothing snaps, jolts, or startles.
- Reduced-motion behavior is defined.
- Animation avoids layout-triggering properties during implementation planning.

## Pixel Art And Mascot Assets

- Mascot work follows Character Design and Mascot Production guides.
- Silhouette, expression, proportions, palette, and emotional readability match the mascot identity.
- Sprite, SVG, frame, export, and naming requirements are satisfied.
- New assets pass Mascot Production QC, Character Checklist, and Brand Checklist.
- No automated or generic asset style overrides the documented character direction.

## Mascot Expressions And Animation

- Expression matches a documented emotional state or a justified extension.
- Behavior communicates through body language, not manipulative text.
- Animation respects the character's personality, timing, and recovery rules.
- Tail, ears, eyes, and posture support the intended emotion.
- Mascot never appears desperate, punitive, guilt-inducing, or off-brand.

## Typography, Color, And Tokens

- Typography follows Brand Book, Visual Design, and UI Production guidance.
- Color choices use documented palette meaning and permitted zones.
- New token requests have a repeated, documented need.
- Text remains readable across supported viewports.
- Color is not the only communicator of state.

## Empty, Error, Loading, And Reward States

- Empty states feel inviting, not lonely or broken.
- Error states feel calm, helpful, and recoverable.
- Loading states use patient, warm feedback.
- Rewards feel like gifts, not slot-machine payouts.
- Mascot behavior in these states matches Brand Book and Character Design guidance.

## Scanner Experience

- Scanner view is the primary focus during scanning.
- Pet presence supports anticipation and delight without blocking scanning.
- Feedback clearly distinguishes scanning, success, failure, and retry.
- Transition back to Home preserves the feeding/care emotional arc.
- Camera and controls remain accessible and responsive.

## Home Screen Composition

- Home Hub keeps the pet as the visual center.
- Room, HUD, navigation, and secondary panels support rather than compete with the pet.
- Idle state feels alive and calm.
- Primary scan action is clear and emotionally integrated.
- Composition remains strong across mobile, tablet, and desktop.

## Accessibility

- Contrast, focus, keyboard navigation, touch targets, labels, and reduced motion pass UI Production guidance.
- Visual states do not rely only on color.
- Motion-heavy moments have accessible alternatives.
- Text size and layout remain readable on supported breakpoints.

## Emotional And Brand Consistency

- Decision reinforces warmth, tenderness, calm, companionship, or delight.
- Brand voice is warm, gentle, observant, and patient.
- Design feels handcrafted and specific to Scan Chan.
- No urgency, guilt, scarcity, aggressive monetization cues, or corporate tone.
- Final result would still be recognizable as Scan Chan without the logo.
